Election Rules/Regulations/Information

1.            All bona fide members of MCA, who register online (details of ‘Registration’ process will inform later) are eligible to cast his/her vote and for contesting in the MCA Election 2015- 16.

2.            The candidates should submit duly filled nomination form online along with a copy of a recent passport size photo within the stipulated date and time to the election email id iitbmca.elections@gmail.com.

3.            The candidates are encouraged to submit one A4 sheet manifesto highlighting their plan of actions. Manifestos are subject to approval by the committee.

4.            The persons who propose/second the candidates should send a mail with their details (Name, Roll No., email id, Hostel and Room No) to the election email id without which the nomination forms will be considered incomplete.

5.            The candidates and the persons who propose and second the candidates should send the concerned mails only to iitbmca.elections@gmail.com from their respective Ldap/gpo id (IITB email account).

6.            Any incomplete nomination form will be immediately rejected.

7.            A member can either propose or second only one candidate.

8.            Candidates cannot either propose or second any candidate.

9.            Any election committee member or the present council member cannot propose or  second a candidate.

10.        Any campaigning material should not contain any offensive / indecent comments /  remarks. Anti campaigning is strictly prohibited.

11.        Campaigning through electronic format via social media is permitted, as long as the campaigning does not violate above said rules(point 10).

12.        The electorate is the MCA members who have done online registration within the  stipulated time limit.

13.        Any form of malpractice by the candidates would lead to cancellation of their  candidature.

A simple majority would decide the result. • Case 1: In case of only one candidate for a post, the number of YES votes should exceed the number of NO votes for the candidate to be declared elected. • Case 2: In case of a post having several contestants, if a candidate obtains the largest number of votes, then s/he will be declared elected. • In Case 1, if the number of NO votes is larger than the number of YES votes, then the post would be declared vacant.

14.        If any of the posts were declared vacant, then the Patron of MCA, along with other elected Council Members would nominate a member for that post.

15.        The decision of the Election Committee will be final and binding on all parties concerned on all matters pertaining to the election.

16.        Violation of any of the rules will lead to disqualification of the concerned (Voters/contestants)

17.        In case of any clarifications/complaints contact the Election committee (iitbmca.elections@gmail.com).
